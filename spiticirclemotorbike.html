Spiti Circle Motorbike Tour

Spiti Valley is a cold desert mountain valley located high in the Himalayas. The name "Spiti" means "The Middle Land", i.e. the land between Tibet and India. Local population follow Buddhism similar to that found in the nearby Tibet and Ladakh regions.
Spiti Circuit is one of the remotest areas of our country and because of this very reason, it was largely unexplored and highly scenic. Bike Tour to remote areas in Spiti & Kinnar regions is an adventure. Because of off-roading, beautiful lakes, culture & Tourism thousands of people across the world have started to visit Spiti & Kinnaur valley.

Itinerary

Day 01: Manali - Chatru over the Rohtang Pass(3980m): 82 km: (4-5hrs)

Day 02: Chatru - Chandratal lake(4250m): 45km: (3-4hrs)

Day 03: Chandratal - Kunzam Pass(4558m) – Kaza(3800m): 96km: (5-6 hrs)

Day 04: In Kaza - Visit the Ki monastery(4166m), and small gompa at Kibber(4205m): 21km

Day 05: Kaza – Lalung(3658m) – Dhankar(3894m) – Tabo(3280m): 90 km: (6hrs)

Day 06: Tabo – Nako(3662m): 69 km: (3hrs)

Day 07: Khajjiar – Dalhousie(1970m)- Dharamshala(1457m): 128kms

Day 08: Dharamshala – Mandi(850m) – Manali: 240kms

Day 06: Nako – Kalpa(2960m): 125 km : (6 hrs)

Day 07: Kalpa – Sangla village(2696m): 51km: (2 hrs)

Day 08: In Sangla - Visit Chitkul Village(3450m)

Day 09: Sangla – Sarahan(2100m) visit Bhimakali temple: 92 km: (4 hrs)

Day 10: Sarahan – Jibhi(1356m): 131km: (5-6hrs)

Day 11: Jibhi to Manali(2050m): 100kms: (5hrs)
